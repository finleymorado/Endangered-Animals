Sumatran Orangutan

Sumatran Orangutans are amongst the three species of orangutan. They are only found on Sumatra, where they live in the tropical rainforests. Sumatran orangutans are known for their intelligence, as well as their red-orange coat and long limbs.

Diet

Sumatran orangutans usually eat fruits, along with leaves, flowers, bark, and insects. They are omnivores, with a flexible diet that can easily adapt to their resources at the moment.


Habitat

Sumatran orangutans live in tropical and subtropical forests. They tend to live in the northern part of Sumatra, located in the Indonesian Islands. They primarily live in the trees.


Critically Endangered

Sumatran orangutans are critically endangered, with only about 14,000 left. This ranking places them among the rankings of the World's Top 25 Most Endangered Primate Species. They are endandered due to habitat loss, which is caused primarily by deforestation. Poaching and other human activities put these orangutans at risk.
