White Rhinoceros

The white rhinoceros, also known as the white rhino or square-lipped rhinoceros, is a large pachyderm with two subspecies, the southern and the northern white rhinoceros. They are known for their wide mouth, used for grazing, and their tendency to be social. They are the most social out of all rhinoceros species.

Diet

White rhinos are herbivores. They primarily graze on grass and low shrubs. This makes them the largest pure grazers in the world. White rhinos have wide mouths that make it very easy to graze grass, and cut it right above the ground. While they mostly prefer grass, they can also eat other leaves, fruits, and vegetation.


Habitat

White rhinos live in the grasslands, savannas, and shrublands of Africa. They tend to be found in Southern Africa, around Namibia, Zimbabwe, and Kenya.


Nearly Threatened

The White rhinoceros is identified as nearly threatened, with around 18,000 rhinos left in protected areas. However, the northern subspecies is critically endandered, with only two left.
